Superstore Analysis

This is an analysis made on a superstore to understand why they had been making losses over a four year period, yet it had accounts on a substantial amount of sales over the same year period.

MatPlotLib Python Sales Data Automation

Overview

Step into the world of a prominent superstore, where everything seemed to be running smoothly — sales were soaring and customers were flooding through the doors. But behind the scenes, a mystery was brewing. Despite these impressive numbers, the store was grappling with a huge financial loss.

I was brought in as a data analyst to uncover the truth. What I found was both surprising and eye-opening. Years of data held the clues — and with careful cleaning, analysis, and storytelling, I unraveled the reasons behind the financial decline and provided a clear roadmap to fix it.

After the initial discovery, I performed in-depth data cleaning to prepare the dataset for analysis:

  • Checked summary statistics to detect inconsistencies or outliers.
  • Removed duplicate records for clean and accurate insights.
  • Added new derived columns for additional context (e.g., profit margins).
  • Applied data imputation, outlier handling, and normalization techniques.

    • By going through this thorough data cleaning process, we were able to uncover more information that would have been obscured by the inconsistencies and inaccuracies in the raw data. And with this newfound clarity, we were able to take the next step in our investigation and bring the superstore's financial struggles to a close.

              • Findings, Recommendations and Conclusions

              • On analyzing the Super Store's sales and profitability data, it was found that the Technology sub-category and specific products such as Phones and Chairs are our highest selling and most profitable items. To offset losses from less profitable products such as Bookcases and Tables, I recommend bundling them with the high-performing items.
              • For customers looking for a home office setup, I suggest creating a package deal that includes a variety of office essentials such as a table, chairs, phone, copiers, storage, labels, fasteners, and bookcases. This is a great option for customers who are busy and may not have the time to select individual products.
              • To address the issue of losses from selling Supplies, Bookcases, and Tables, I recommend considering dropping these products from our catalog or renegotiating with suppliers for a cheaper price.
              • Our customer base is primarily made up of Consumer and Corporate segments, which make up over 70% of our customers. To target these segments, I suggest offering special promotions and bundles for mass Consumer and Home Offices in the top 10 cities with the highest sales, and sending promotional emails or flyers to customers in the East and West regions.
              • The main reason for losses is discounts. To improve this, I recommend offering more discounts during festival seasons, as this will result in more sales. During these seasons, the discount rate is recommended to be not more than 20% as more than a 20% discount brings in great losses.
              • Additionally, the home office segment needs improvement. In some cities, we have fewer sales, which may be due to a lack of awareness. Advertising in these cities may help increase sales. For advertising strategy, in the event that the company wants to run product package promos, it is advised that the most common items that are usually bought together be used to inform the company's decision.

              Analysis of Udemy Web Application Data

              The objective of this project is to clean and preprocess the WebDevelopment.csv dataset using the pandas library in Python. The dataset contains information about web development courses from different websites.

              Python Data Cleaning Data Science ML

              Cleaning and Preprocessing Web Development Course Data

              Project Objective

              The goal of this project was to prepare a raw dataset of web development courses for meaningful analysis by performing comprehensive data cleaning and preprocessing using Python and the pandas library. The dataset, originally obtained from Kaggle, consists of 1,200 rows and 16 columns, each representing course attributes such as pricing, duration, learner engagement, and platform-specific metadata. By applying robust cleaning techniques, we ensured the dataset was analysis-ready and free from inconsistencies, irrelevant columns, and misformatted values.

              This project also aims to establish a repeatable and reusable data preparation pipeline. The resulting clean dataset will serve as a foundational asset for future exploratory analysis, business insights generation, and even potential machine learning applications such as pricing optimization or popularity forecasting.

              Dataset Overview

              The dataset includes information about web development courses offered across various online learning platforms. Below is a detailed breakdown of the columns:

              • id: Unique identifier for each course
              • title: Title of the course
              • url: Hosting website or course URL
              • isPaid: Indicates if the course is paid (TRUE) or free (FALSE)
              • price: The course price in USD
              • numSubscribers: Number of users enrolled in the course
              • numReviews: Total reviews submitted by users
              • numPublishedLectures: Number of lectures included in the course
              • instructionalLevel: Difficulty level (All Levels, Beginner, Intermediate, Expert)
              • contentInfo (mins): Duration of the course, to be standardized to minutes
              • publishedTime: Date the course was published
              • Unnamed: 11 to Unnamed: 15: Empty or irrelevant columns to be removed

                • Technologies & Tools Used

                • Language: Python 3.9.7
                • Libraries: pandas 1.3.3, numpy
                • Platform: Jupyter Notebook / Google Colab
                • Source: Kaggle

                  • Data Cleaning & Preprocessing Steps

                  A structured and meticulous cleaning pipeline was followed to ensure data integrity and usability. The main steps included:

                  • Initial Inspection: Loaded the dataset using pd.read_csv(), followed by df.head(), df.shape, and df.info() to inspect dimensions, structure, and null values.
                  • Removing Irrelevant Columns: Dropped Unnamed: 11 through Unnamed: 15 which contained no usable data.
                  • Standardizing Boolean Fields: Ensured the isPaid column only contained 'TRUE' or 'FALSE' to clearly distinguish free vs paid courses.
                  • Cleaning Price Field: Converted all prices from string format (e.g. '$19.99') to numerical values. Handled 'Free' as 0 and corrected any negative values to absolute values. Used str.replace() and astype() for transformation.
                  • Fixing Review Counts: Converted all values in numReviews to positive integers to reflect actual user activity and avoid inconsistencies.
                  • Normalizing Instruction Levels: Standardized the instructionalLevel column by mapping inconsistent text cases and phrasing to one of four levels: All, Beginner, Intermediate, or Expert.
                  • Standardizing Course Duration: Transformed content duration to minutes (if given in hours) using string manipulation and regex patterns to extract numeric values. For example, '2.5 hours' was converted to 150 minutes.
                  • Export Clean Dataset: The cleaned dataset was exported to a new CSV file using df.to_csv("Clean_WebDev.csv", index=False) for future use.

                    • Final Output & Results

                    After cleaning, the dataset was transformed into a refined structure containing 11 relevant columns with consistent and valid values across all rows. There were no remaining nulls or inconsistencies. The dataset is now ready for:

                    • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on pricing, course length, and user engagement
                    • Segmentation of courses by level or price tier
                    • Training machine learning models (e.g., price prediction, popularity forecasting)
                    • Building dashboards or visual reports with tools like Tableau or Power BI

                      • Key Takeaways

                      • Effective data cleaning is essential for accurate downstream analysis.
                      • Real-world datasets often contain messy fields and irrelevant columns that need domain-driven decisions to clean.
                      • Automation using pandas allows efficient transformations even on large datasets.
                      • Clear documentation of each step supports reproducibility and collaboration with other analysts.
